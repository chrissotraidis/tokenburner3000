# Arcade Spectacle Flow

1. Lobby opens on the token singularity with calm idle motion.
2. Pointer movement shifts parallax depth; the overload meter and live ticker
   establish motion without blocking the primary action.
3. Entering the arena accelerates the transition into fighter select.
4. Hovering, focusing, or selecting a roster entry updates the featured fighter,
   stat bars, lore, and corner treatment.
5. With two fighters locked, arena select opens on a visual stage carousel.
6. Highlighting an arena swaps the environment and explains its real scoring
   modifiers before confirmation.
7. Tale of the Tape becomes a face-off. The user may file a prediction and press
   `HYPE` for a presentation-only surge.
8. The fight begins with both transcript panes readable over the chosen stage.
9. Scheduled events, lead changes, audience actions, and countdown phases update
   momentum, crowd, background intensity, and camera treatment.
10. The verdict retains the chosen stage and uses tabbed internal panels.

## Failure and Recovery

- If generated art fails, the existing black cabinet surfaces and colors remain.
- If WebGL cannot start, static raster backdrops and DOM status meters remain.
- If effects are reduced, parallax, shake, rapid flicker, and particle motion stop.
- If the viewport is compact, fighters stack and arena previews become a
  contained horizontal carousel; primary actions stay visible.
